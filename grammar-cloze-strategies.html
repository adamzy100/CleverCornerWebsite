Grammar Cloze Strategies for PSLE English

A practical, step-by-step approach to tackling the Grammar Cloze in PSLE English Paper 2 — including how to identify the right word type for each blank and avoid common traps.

📋 2025 SEAB syllabus aligned 🎯 10 blanks · 10 marks

What Is Grammar Cloze?

Grammar Cloze is found in Booklet B of Paper 2. Students are given a passage with 10 blanks, each worth 1 mark (total: 10 marks). Unlike the Comprehension Cloze which tests vocabulary, Grammar Cloze tests your child's ability to use grammar accurately — supplying the correct grammatical form of a word to complete each sentence meaningfully.

The blanks typically test: verb tenses, articles (a/an/the), prepositions, conjunctions, pronouns, and auxiliary verbs.

Grammar Cloze vs. Comprehension Cloze — what's the difference?

Grammar Cloze tests grammar rules (the form of words). The answer is determined by grammatical rules — there is usually one correct answer.

Comprehension Cloze tests vocabulary (the meaning of words), grammar skills and ability to pick out contextual clues.

Step-by-Step Strategy

1

Read the entire passage first

Before attempting any blank, read the whole passage to understand what it is about — the topic, tone, and time frame (past, present, or future). This prevents you from filling in blanks without context and then having to change your answers later.

2

Identify the tense of the passage

Look at the sentences before and after each blank. What tense is used? Are there time signal words like yesterday, every day, last year, since, or tomorrow? Confirm the tense before filling in any verb blank.

3

Determine what word type is needed

Before guessing the word, ask: what type of word fits here — a verb, article, preposition, conjunction, pronoun, or adverb? The words around the blank are strong clues. See the word type guide below.

4

Apply the grammar rule

Once you know the word type, apply the relevant rule. For example: if the blank needs a verb after was, it is likely a continuous tense (-ing form). If it follows has or have, it is a past participle. Use the rule — don't just guess.

5

Read the sentence back with your answer

Once you've filled in your answer, re-read the sentence aloud in your head. Does it sound grammatically correct? Does it make sense in the context of the passage? If something feels off, revisit your grammar rule before moving on.

Common Word Types Tested in Grammar Cloze

Most Grammar Cloze blanks fall into one of these categories. Learn the clues for each:

Verb Tense
The right form of the verb
Look for: time signal words, auxiliary verbs before the blank (was, have, will), or the tense used in surrounding sentences.
walkedis runninghas eatenwill go
Articles
a / an / the
First mention of a noun → a/an. Specific or previously mentioned noun → the. General concepts → no article.
a dogan applethe park
Prepositions
in / on / at / of / for / with…
Look at what follows the blank (a time, place, or person). Learn fixed preposition pairings with common adjectives and verbs.
in 2024on Mondayat schoolafraid of
Conjunctions
Linking words
Look at the relationship between the two clauses — cause/effect (because), contrast (although/but), addition (and), condition (if/unless).
althoughbecauseunlesswhile
Pronouns
he / she / it / they / whom…
Identify what noun is being referred to. Check if the blank is subject or object position to determine the correct pronoun form.
he / himthey / themwho / whom
Auxiliaries
Modal & helping verbs
Look for the main verb that follows. Modals express ability (can), permission (may), obligation (must), or prediction (will/would).
couldshouldmightwas / were

Practice Example

Grammar Cloze — Practice Passage
Last Saturday, the Lee family 1. went to the Botanic Gardens for a picnic. They 2. had been planning the outing for two weeks. When they arrived, they found 3. a shady spot under a large tree. Mrs Lee spread 4. the picnic mat on the grass while Mr Lee unpacked the food. The children ran around excitedly 5. until their mother called them over to eat.
Answers explained: 1. went — simple past (time signal: "Last Saturday") · 2. had — past perfect (action completed before another past action) · 3. a — indefinite article (first mention, consonant sound) · 4. the — definite article (specific mat they brought) · 5. until — conjunction showing duration of action

Most common Grammar Cloze pitfalls

  • Filling in a present tense verb when the passage is clearly in the past
  • Writing "a" before a vowel sound (e.g., "a apple" → should be "an apple")
  • Using "in" or "at" incorrectly with time expressions (in the morning / at night / on Monday)
  • Forgetting that "was" needs -ing (was running) but "had" needs past participle (had run)
  • Leaving blanks empty — always attempt every blank
