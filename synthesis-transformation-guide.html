PSLE Synthesis & Transformation: A Complete Guide

All 6 question types explained with grammar rules, worked examples, and practice questions — based on the 2025 SEAB syllabus. No partial marks, so every rule counts.

📋 2025 SEAB syllabus ⚠️ 5 questions · 2 marks each · No partial marks

What You Need to Know First

Synthesis and Transformation is found in Booklet B of Paper 2. There are 5 questions, each worth 2 marks — making it a 10-mark section. Crucially, there are no partial marks: every part of the answer must be correct to score. A single grammatical error means zero for that question.

⚠️ No partial marks — what this means for your child

Unlike comprehension or grammar cloze where one blank is one mark, each S&T question is all-or-nothing. Getting 4 out of 5 correct gives 8/10, but a careless tense change in the last word of a reported speech answer can cost the full 2 marks for that question. Accuracy and checking are everything.

The 6 Question Types

Type 1
Combining with Conjunctions
Join two sentences using a conjunction that shows the correct relationship — cause/effect (because), contrast (although/but/even though), addition (and/not only…but also), time (when/while/after/before), or condition (if/unless).
Original
She was tired. She stayed up to finish her homework.
✓ Transformed (keyword: although)
Although she was tired, she stayed up to finish her homework.
Watch out: When using although/even though, place a comma after the subordinate clause if it comes first. Meaning must not change — do not add or remove ideas.
Type 2
Relative Clauses (who / which / that)
Combine two sentences about the same person or thing using a relative pronoun. Use who for people, which for things/animals, and that for either (in defining clauses).
Original
The man is a famous chef. He appeared on TV last night.
✓ Transformed (keyword: who)
The man who appeared on TV last night is a famous chef.
Watch out: Check the position of the relative clause — it should be placed immediately after the noun it describes. Removing the subject pronoun (he, she, it) from the relative clause is required.
Type 3
Reported (Indirect) Speech
Change direct speech (with quotation marks) into reported speech. Remove quotation marks, use that, change pronouns (I→he/she), and backshift tenses (is→was, will→would, am→was, can→could).
Original
Mary said, "I will visit you tomorrow."
✓ Transformed (keyword: that)
Mary said that she would visit me the next day.
Key tense changes: am/is/are → was/were · will → would · can → could · may → might · "tomorrow" → the next day · "today" → that day · "yesterday" → the previous day
Type 4
Active and Passive Voice
In active voice, the subject performs the action. In passive voice, the subject receives the action. Structure: Object + was/were/is/are + past participle + by + subject.
Original (Active)
The teacher marked the essays.
✓ Transformed (keyword: by)
The essays were marked by the teacher.
Watch out: Match the tense of to be (was/were/is/are/has been) to the original. The object becomes the new subject. Use the past participle — not simple past — of the verb.
Type 5
If-Conditionals
At PSLE level, the main conditional tested is the First Conditional (real/possible situations): If + present simple, will + base verb. Also tested: Unless (meaning "if not").
Original
You do not hurry. You will miss the bus.
✓ Transformed (keyword: If)
If you do not hurry, you will miss the bus.
Original
You do not study. You will fail.
✓ Transformed (keyword: Unless)
Unless you study, you will fail.
Remember: Use a comma after the if/unless clause when it comes first. Unless = If…not, so do not use "not" again in the clause.
Type 6
Inversion and Emphasis
For emphasis, move a negative adverb (Never, Seldom, Rarely, Little) or a limiting expression (Not only, Hardly) to the front, then invert the subject and auxiliary verb.
Original
I have never tasted such delicious food before.
✓ Transformed (keyword: Never)
Never have I tasted such delicious food before.
Structure: Negative adverb + auxiliary verb + subject + main verb. The most commonly tested at PSLE: Never have I… · Seldom did he… · Rarely does she…

4-step checking routine

  • Step 1: Identify the question type from the given keyword
  • Step 2: Apply the correct grammar structure for that type
  • Step 3: Check that the meaning is the same as the two original sentences — no ideas added or removed
  • Step 4: Check grammar details: tense, subject-verb agreement, pronouns, and punctuation (comma placement)
